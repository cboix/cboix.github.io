I am a postdoc at Harvard Medical School working for Shamil Sunyaev.

I develop computational approaches, methods, and resources to study the genetic and molecular underpinnings of human disease, with a focus on neurodegeneration. I am particularly interested in understanding the impacts of genetic variation across a spectrum of varying molecular and cellular contexts. My research interests include:

  • Mapping molecular mechanisms to endophenotypes in a context-specific manner
  • Progression, resilience, and vulnerability to neurodegeneration
  • Convergence of disease-associated variation onto common mechanisms
  • Proximal mechanisms of disease-associated variation
  • Learning structured representations of molecular processes

    • Before this, I received my Ph.D. from MIT under the supervision of Manolis Kellis in Computational and Systems Biology, where I studied disease progression and the role of DNA damage in neurodegeneration (with Li-Huei Tsai) and developed epigenomic references and methods for understanding gene regulation and complex disease. I received my undergraduate degree from Princeton University, where I worked with John Storey on statistics for high-dimensional genomic data.
